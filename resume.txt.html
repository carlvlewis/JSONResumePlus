About

I'm an interactive/data journalist, civic-tech entrepreneur, newsroom change consultant, part-time digital journalism educator, design thinker, former online editor/social media manager, mobile/front-end designer/developer, interaction programmer, Wordpress/Drupal dev., Stanford-certified project manager and aspiring data scientist, with more than seven years of cumulative experience in interactive storytelling, data visualization, audience engagement, social media/email marketing strategy, web programming, community-building, UX best-practices, digital content production, deadline reporting and digital analytics/social media metrics. Recent skill-sets I've dabbled in include Python, R, data science, statistical modeling and geospatial visualizations, as well as educational technology and digital security. My overarching goal – whether in journalism, data visualization, information design, marketing, mobile development or, most recently, entrepreneurship – has always been motivated by the goal of building stronger, more informed communities, locally and globally. I'm an urbanist who believes that diversity, digital transparency, social mobility, civic discourse, an informed public, and communal values are the key to our sustainable future – whether that be in Macon, Ga., or midtown Manhattan. Nothing brings me more joy than teaching and training journalists and employees how to incorporate data into their narratives to help expose hidden stories.
